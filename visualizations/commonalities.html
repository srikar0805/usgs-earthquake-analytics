Earthquake Commonalities (Same-Day Co-Occurrences)

Antecedent (A): The originating grid cell where an earthquake occurred on a given day.
Consequent (B): Another grid cell that experienced an earthquake on the same day as A.
Line thickness represents confidence; node size and color indicate support.

Each bar represents a grid cell (latitude, longitude) showing how often earthquakes occurred there — measured as the fraction of days with at least one event. Higher support = more frequent activity.

Each row represents a same-day co-occurrence rule between two grid cells.
Antecedent (A): originating cell; Consequent (B): co-occurring cell.
Support: frequency of A & B occurring together.
Confidence: P(A & B) / P(A).
Lift: ratio of observed co-occurrence to expected independence.
